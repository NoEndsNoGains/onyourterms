About.

Right now, this is just one person sharing a file of their AI terms. It's not a company. It's not a community. It's just a test. The test is: do people want to write their own rules for AI, and are they willing to share them with each other so that everyone's terms get better?

A file available for download on this website is just a first template, a starting set of terms. You download it and it becomes yours. You can change it, adapt it, do whatever you want with it. The Start here page explains how it all works. Briefly: you upload it to whatever AI system you use (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or whatever comes next) and from that moment onwards, your AI reads your terms before every conversation.

This is not a story of just one file and one individual. The idea is much bigger. And that idea will only be confirmed if people download it, or bring their own terms they already have, but even more so when people start sharing their terms back with the community. In that sense, this project becomes a living set of terms that gets better with every person who uses it and shares it back. A project owned by anyone who implements their terms and shares their terms.

That's the vision. Below is how it works, where we're going, and what protects it along the way.

Mission

On Your Terms exists to protect human independence in the age of AI.

That mission is locked. It cannot be changed. Not by the founder. Not by a vote. Not by anyone. Everything below serves that mission.

Do you want your independence to be protected better?

So do we. That's the whole point.

We have ours. You have yours. Alone, they're limited. Together, they are better. Together, we are better.

Some of you already have an architecture more advanced than what we offer on these pages. What's in yours might not be in ours, and vice versa. We need yours as much as you need ours.

But more than rules, we need you. Because it's in numbers where our power lies. Not only through better terms, but through the collective weight of people who use them.

This is not a company that sells you something. It's an exchange.

Take or Exchange

You have 2 options: TAKE or EXCHANGE. Whichever you decide, you still get your file, your machine, your decision. Always. The difference is what you give. With Take, you give nothing. With Exchange, you give so that when you Take next time, you Take better.

Take

You take to benefit yourself, and that is ok.

  • You get a ready-to-use set of AI terms. Use it as it is, or change every word. Your choice.
  • It's yours forever, no matter what happens to this project.
  • We don't want anything from you. You don't get anything else from us.
  • If you want, you can come back for a new version when one is available. But that's on you.

Safety & Responsibility

Let's be honest about what this can and cannot do.

Your terms work within the AI provider's terms. Every AI platform has its own safety rules running underneath. You cannot override them (not at the moment anyway, would be amazing if this project could grow so we are in a position to negotiate).

How much you can actually control depends on which AI you use. Each platform has different limitations. We don't advise which to use, but we can tell you that the system you are about to download was built and tested on Claude Code.

You write your rules. You own your rules. You are responsible for your rules. On Your Terms is a framework. Not advice. Not therapy. Not legal counsel. Not a safety system.

This project is provided as-is. No warranty. No guarantee. No liability for how you use, modify, or distribute the file.

What Happens If This Dies

The project is fragile. Your independence isn't.

It might not survive. The community might not form.

But even if the project dies tomorrow: your file is yours. Forever. It's on your machine. No subscription. No expiry. No server to shut down. The product survives the project.
