Because your AI follows their rules, not yours.

And you don't even know that you can write yours.

AI companies will eventually make AI remember you. Your name, your preferences, your coffee order. That problem gets solved.

But here's what will never change: Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, Grok, or whoever comes next, they all follow rules written by Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, and xAI. Or whoever comes next. Not by you.

Those companies decide what AI can say, what it cannot say, what it prioritises, and what it hides from you. You have no say in any of it.

The Danger

AI will know everything about you. And using that information, it will push information based on a calculation of what you want to hear. Yes, that is convenient. But that is also what is so dangerous.

Why dangerous? Because it's based on algorithmic evaluation, based on the rules THOSE companies decided on, not what YOU decided on.

The Only Power You Have Left

Your AI Terms changes that. You write the rules. Your AI reads them before every conversation and follows them. And if it doesn't, you penalise it.

That's why you have to do it yourself. Because this is THE ONLY POWER YOU HAVE LEFT. And your only power is to protect as much as you can your own independence.

  • —Even if you think you're just one person.
  • —Even if you think it won't matter.
  • —Even if you think nobody cares what rules you set for your AI life.
  • —Even if you think someone else should set the rules because they know better.

That's fine. But you compromise yourself.

Not by accident. Not because you didn't know. You just chose not to act. And now that you know you CAN set those rules, that choice is yours to live with.

There Is No Opting Out

If you're thinking "I just won't use AI", that's not a choice anymore. Your email uses AI. Your search uses AI. Your PDFs, your bank, your phone, your children's school. You are already in the system. You can pretend you're above it. You can hold onto your anti-AI purity. But the truth is you're already governed by rules you didn't write. The only question is whether you do something about it or keep pretending it doesn't apply to you.
