What you get

One file.
Your rules.

A governance framework for your relationship with AI. Not a plugin. Not an app. A text file, built on a template shared by the community, that you own completely and adapt as your own.

What's inside

Six sections. All yours to change.

Your Bill of RightsThirteen rights you hold in every AI interaction, from the right to disconnect to the right to cognitive sovereignty. You keep, change, or add to every one of them.
How Your Brain WorksThe section most AI tools never ask about. How you process information. What you need. What derails you. What helps you think. This is what makes the framework yours.
Communication RulesHow you want the AI to talk to you. Short or long. Direct or exploratory. What it should never do. What it should always do. Written in plain language.
Your ProjectsA list of what you're working on so AI knows your context without you re-explaining every session.
MemoryA log of each session. Your AI reads it next time. No conversation starts from zero.
How We LearnA learning loop. Mistakes are logged. What works is captured. Each session teaches the next.

Your AI Terms file is the base layer. It applies to everything. For specific projects, add project rules on top. The base layer is always active underneath.

Where does it go

Works on any AI.

Built and tested on Claude Code. Works anywhere your AI lets you give it instructions.

Claude CodeSave as CLAUDE.md in your project folder. It reads it automatically every session.
Claude chatPaste into a Project's custom instructions, or upload at the start of a conversation.
ChatGPTPaste into Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions. Or upload as a file.
GeminiPaste into a Gem's instructions, or upload at the start of a conversation.
Any other AIIf it has a custom instructions field or lets you upload files, it works. Copy, paste, go.

Multiple AIs

One file. Every AI.

Your terms work on all of them. One master copy on your computer. Start every session by uploading the latest. At the end, ask: "What changed in my terms this session?" Save changes back.

The file already tells your AI to flag changes at session end. One master, many AIs, always in sync.

When the AI fails

You have a system. Not a complaint.

When the AI breaks a rule, you log the failure. You examine the rule. Was it unclear? Was it addressing the wrong mechanism? You strengthen it. You try again.

Three failures on the same root cause and you know the approach needs to change, not just the wording. The failure becomes the test case for a stronger version.

A document that has been violated and repaired is more robust than one that has never been tested. Violations make your terms stronger, not weaker.
