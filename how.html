What you get.

You get the example of someone's terms, made publicly available to everyone. It's a framework — a starting point to upload to any AI you will ever use.

Five sections. One file. (v0.1)

  • Your Rules Pre-written rules about what your AI can and cannot do. Edit any of them.
  • Who You Are All about you. What you want AI to know — and where you draw the line of what it should not remember or know.
  • Your Projects A list of your projects so AI (any you plug your architecture into) knows your context without you re-explaining every session.
  • What Happened A log of what happened in each session. Your AI reads it next time so you never start from zero.
  • How We Learn A learning loop. Each session teaches the next.

What it gives you.

  • POWER Rights declared by you, for any AI to respect.
  • SMARTER AI knows your rules, your context, and who you are — within the limits you set. Every conversation starts better.
  • MEMORY AI remembers you. No conversation starts from zero — unless you tell it to forget.
  • PORTABLE Works on any AI. Upload it to anything.
  • ALIVE Your file evolves. What works stays. What doesn't gets fixed.
  • SIMPLE Just a text file.

Four steps. Two minutes.

1. Download the file. It's a text file. Like a Word document but simpler.

2. Give it to your AI. Drop the file where your AI can see it, or paste the contents into your AI's custom instructions.

3. Your AI walks you through it. Each section asks: "keep this or change it?" You decide as you go.

4. Start talking. Your AI now reads your terms before every conversation. It knows who you are and what you expect. Not every rule will be followed perfectly — but every conversation starts from your terms, not theirs.

Where does the file go?

This framework was built and tested on Claude Code — that's where it works best. But it works on any AI that lets you give it instructions. Here's how:

  • Claude Code — save as CLAUDE.md in your project folder. It reads it automatically every session.
  • Claude chat — paste into a Project's custom instructions, or upload the file at the start of a conversation.
  • ChatGPT — paste into Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions. Or upload as a file in any conversation.
  • Gemini — paste into a Gem's instructions, or upload at the start of a conversation.
  • Any other AI — if it has a custom instructions field or lets you upload files, it works. Copy, paste, go.

You use more than one AI. So does your file.

Claude today, ChatGPT tomorrow, Manus next week. Your terms work on all of them. But if each AI evolves your file independently, you end up with different versions and lose control.

The rule: one file, one master copy, every platform gets the latest.

  • 1. Keep one master copy on your computer.
  • 2. Start every session by uploading the latest version.
  • 3. At the end, ask your AI: "What changed in my terms this session?"
  • 4. Copy changes back to your master file.

The file already tells your AI to flag changes at session end. You just need to save them back. One master, many AIs, always in sync.
