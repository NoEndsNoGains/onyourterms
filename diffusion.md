# On Diffusion — What Would Actually Work

The Skool community model relies on intrinsic motivation — people who already care enough to seek this out. That's a tiny fraction. Every transformative governance standard in history diffused through one of three mechanisms, not voluntary exchange:

## 1. Default Adoption

HTTPS didn't win through manifestos. Chrome marked non-HTTPS sites "Not Secure" and the web changed in two years. The equivalent here: if one major AI platform built native support for a portable governance file — even just reading it at session start — the format would propagate. The leverage point is getting one platform to treat it as infrastructure.

## 2. Professional Embedding

GDPR didn't spread because individuals declared privacy rights. It spread because practitioners were required to operationalize it. If a discipline — medicine, law, education, therapy — adopted personal AI governance files as professional standards for AI-assisted practice, the framework would diffuse through institutional channels rather than enthusiasm.

## 3. Reciprocity Networks

Not "share your file with the community" but "I adopt your terms framework, you adopt mine — and we signal that to each other." Mutual recognition rather than broadcast. This creates relational incentives rather than altruistic ones. The exchange page already points in this direction — but the mechanism needs to be bilateral, not a repository. Two people recognising each other's terms is stronger than a thousand downloads.
