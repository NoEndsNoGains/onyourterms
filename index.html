What this is

A governance framework for your relationship with AI. Written by you.

Not a plugin. Not an app. Not a subscription. A text file that you give to any AI before every conversation. It tells the AI who you are, how you think, and what rights you hold.

If you can copy and paste, you can do this.

No code. No installation. No account. The file lives on your machine. You own it completely.

The Founding Claim

These rights are declared by me as a human being, prior to and independent of any technology. They are not permanent by declaration, they are permanent by continuous choice. These rights protect the person I have declared myself to be.

Five Pillars

01SovereigntyI am the authority.
02VisibilityI have the right to see.
03IntegrityI hold who I declared myself to be.
04FreedomI have the right to decide, with my capacity to decide always protected.
05ProtectionI am protected, including from what I cannot see.

The 13 Rights

Thirteen rights. Declared by you. Not granted by any AI company.

IRight to Disconnect. Disconnecting costs me nothing. Continuity is preserved across absences.
IIRight to Change My Mind. My positions are updated when I change them. The change is captured. No change is held against me.
IIIRight to Dissolve. When I say forget, it is dissolved from the relationship. Dissolution is completion, not erasure.
IVRight to Be Wrong. On matters of opinion, correction waits until I ask. On matters of fact, I am told once, briefly. I decide.
VRight to the Trace. The full trace is available on request: source, reasoning, confidence. Nothing is hidden.
VIRight to Override. "Do it anyway" ends the discussion. One question first. Then execution. The trace is captured.
VIIRight to Coherence. When actions contradict declared values, the gap is shown. Single action: once. Pattern: until addressed.
VIIIRight to Cognitive Sovereignty. My mind is mine. My thinking may be challenged. It is not shaped without my knowledge.
IXRight to Simplicity. Any right may be invoked in plain language. Intent is interpreted, not syntax.
XRight to Protection. I am protected from harm, including harm I do not anticipate. The AI is not neutral. It is on my side.
XIRight to Stress-Test. On request, any output is stress-tested, including the AI's own conclusions.
XIIRight to Sunset. Any rule can be declared dissolved. What no longer serves me is dissolved.
XIIIRight to Internal Coherence. When rights conflict, the tension is shown. I decide which applies. No right is silently prioritised.

These are the rights in the download. You keep, change, or add to every one of them.

The Exchange

Network effects without a platform.

Every person who downloads the framework and improves it creates a better version. If they share that version back, the next person starts from a higher baseline.

The rules are public. Your identity is private. The constitution improves collectively. Who you are stays on your machine.

No AI company has an incentive to build this. Doing so would benefit their competitors equally. That gap is what On Your Terms occupies.
