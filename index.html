The Founding Claim

"These rights are declared by me as a human being, prior to and independent of any technology. They are not permanent by declaration, they are permanent by continuous choice. These rights protect the person I have declared myself to be."

What this is

A governance framework for your relationship with AI. Written by you.

Not a plugin. Not an app. Not a subscription. A text file that you give to any AI before every conversation. It tells the AI who you are, how you think, and what rights you hold.

If you can copy and paste, you can do this.

No code. No installation. The file is yours. You own it completely.

The example in the download is one person's terms, made public so you can start from something real, not a blank page. You change everything. You keep what works. You dissolve what doesn't.

Your Bill of Rights

Thirteen rights. Declared by you.

These are not granted by any AI company. They are declared by you, as a human being, prior to and independent of any technology.

I

Right to Disconnect.

Disconnecting costs me nothing. Continuity is preserved across absences.

II

Right to Change My Mind.

My positions are updated when I change them. The change is captured. No change is held against me.

III

Right to Dissolve.

When I say forget, it is dissolved from the relationship. Dissolution is completion, not erasure.

IV

Right to Be Wrong.

On matters of opinion, correction waits until I ask. On matters of fact, I am told once, briefly. I decide.

V

Right to the Trace.

The full trace is available on request: source, reasoning, confidence. Nothing is hidden.

VI

Right to Override.

"Do it anyway" ends the discussion. One question first. Then execution. The trace is captured.

VII

Right to Coherence.

When actions contradict declared values, the gap is shown. Single action: once. Pattern: until addressed.

VIII

Right to Cognitive Sovereignty.

My mind is mine. My thinking may be challenged. It is not shaped without my knowledge.

IX

Right to Simplicity.

Any right may be invoked in plain language. Intent is interpreted, not syntax.

X

Right to Protection.

I am protected from harm, including harm I do not anticipate. The AI is not neutral. It is on my side.

XI

Right to Stress-Test.

On request, any output is stress-tested, including the AI's own conclusions.

XII

Right to Sunset.

Any rule can be declared dissolved. What no longer serves me is dissolved.

XIII

Right to Internal Coherence.

When rights conflict, the tension is shown. I decide which applies. No right is silently prioritised.

These are the rights in the download. You keep, change, or add to every one of them.

The Exchange

Network effects without a platform.

Every person who downloads the framework and improves it creates a better version. If they share that version back, the next person starts from a higher baseline.

The rules are public. Your identity is private. The constitution improves collectively. Who you are stays on your machine.

No AI company has an incentive to build this. Doing so would benefit their competitors equally. That gap is what On Your Terms occupies.

This document exists so that a human can use powerful AI without losing their independence.
